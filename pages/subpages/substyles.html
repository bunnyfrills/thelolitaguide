Lolita Substyles

Throughout its history, Lolita Fashion has seen the development of a variety of styles. Many are now very uncommon to see, but some are making revivals--especially Old School Lolita. Below are styles along with descriptions and brands that typically create items for them. All images listed are taken from Tokyo Fashion's archive. Tip: If you're looking for a specific style, hit ctrl+f and type in a keyword related to it. That will help you find specific information faster.

Sweet Lolita

One of the three most popular substyles. It typically features pastel colors, and cutesy motifs like ponies, carousels, bears, bunnies, candy, fruits, and more. Popular brands include Angelic pretty and Baby the Stars Shine Bright.

Classic Lolita

One of the three most popular substyles. It tends to be more historically based than other styles (especially on the victorian era), and is typically more mature appearing than other styles. Popular brands include Victorian Maiden, Innocent World, and Mary Magdelene.

Gothic Lolita

One of the three most popular substyles. While gothic lolita often includes prints, it is most often created in solid pieces with patterns drafted to embody elegance and sometimes mystique. Popular brands inlclude Moi-Meme-Moitie, Sheglit, Atelier Boz, and Atelier Pierrot.

Shiro Lolita

Meaning "White Lolita," it is style that is made up (almost) entirely of white items only.

Kuro Lolita

Meaning "Black Lolita," it is style that is made up (almost) entirely of black items only.
