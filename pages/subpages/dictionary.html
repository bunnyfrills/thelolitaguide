Lolita Terms Dictionary

Alice (designer): designer who used to work for Moitie. She was the mind behind a lot of iconic Moitie pieces, such as the Rose Cross JSK
A-line: skirts and petticoats that are shaped like an uppercase A.
Barette: small head accessories using a clasp.
Behind the bows: a forum used to post confessions, gossip, and opinions anonymously. Now defunct.
bodice: the top part of the dress that accomodates your bust (and often waist).
bloomers: pumpkin pants/shorts that go under your skirt. Used most often in Old School lolita, or when a dress is too short.
bustle: layers of gathered ruffles in-between two parts of a skirt. They are usually found on the back side of a skirt or dress, but are sometimes found in the front or side.
Canotier: Circular headdress that is tied around the chin and usually rests at a tilted position on the upper head.
CGL: 4chan forum for lolita. Very toxic with a tendency to post others' coords to make fun of. Sometimes leaks related to releases happen there. Not a good representation of the community, a lot of people on there don't even wear the fashion.
cage skirt: kind of like a petticoat, but instead of using ruffles of fabric to give volume to a skirt, it uses stiff hoops. Also referred to as crinolines and hoop skirts. Recommended to be worn instead of pettticoats during the summer and in areas that tend to be hotter.
Colorway: the color version of a dress. For example, Happy Garland has several colorways, including pink, sax, yellow, and white. comm: short for community, they are local communities of lolitas that meet up often while wearing the fashion.
comm meet: an event hosted by at least one (local) lolita community member, in which everyone meets up while wearing the fashion.
cutsew: a blouse that uses t-shirt like material. Often used in casual lolita.
decora: J-fashion style that uses an over abundance of accessories, layering, and colors. Sometimes combined with lolita, usually in the form of wearing many head and wrist accessories.
dir en grey: Visual Kei band formed in 1997
dream dress: Someone's favorite dress that they are searching for on the secondhand market. People are usually willing to pay above retail or market price for this item.
EGL: Elegant and Gothic Lolita
Empire Waist: a dress with a waist that ends at the end of your bust.
fairykei: J-fashion style focused on pastels and nostalgic motifs.
fukubukuro: japanaese for lucky pack.
headdress: head accessory usually in the shape of a wide oval or rectangle.
ISO: In Search Of, refering to a piece a buyer is looking for on the secondhand market.
Jumperskirt: sleeveless dress, most often with a blouse under. Abbreviated as JSK.
Kamikaze Girls: novel and movie adaption that featured a lolita main character.
Kira Imai: popular lolita illustrator. She has created several prints for brands, including Cat Tea Party
Kumya: One of Baby the Stars Shine Bright's mascots, who is a bear.
lucky bag: A bag of random items from a brand that sells for cheaper than the items' combined retail value, usually sold at the end of the year or at the beginning of the new year. Other times, they are bags containing special sets exclusive to those packs. It is not recommended to buy the randomized ones, as they usually contain whatever stock did not sell well (AKA something you are unlikely to want) that year.
Malice Mizer: Visual Kei band that was together from 1992 to 2001. Many original and current lolitas were/are big fans of them.
Mana: Founder of Moi-Meme-Moitie, former member of Malice Mizer, and founder of Moi Dix Mois. Very popular lolita, and he still models for Moitie.
Maxi pad: Comedic term for larger rectangular sized headdresses due to their resemblance to a maxi pad.
Milky-chan: cute fawn character created by Angelic Pretty. She is featured on prints like this.
Moi Dix Mois: Mana's current band.
NWT: New With Tags, it indicates the item has not been thoroughly worn before, and is practically brand new.
Onepiece: dress with sleeves. Abbreviated as OP.
OTK: Over the Knee Socks
OTT: Over the Top, describing lolita that uses more layers, accessories, more intricate and detailed items, and often bigger petticoats.
petticoat: item used under skirts to give them volume.
Polyester: synthetic type of fabric made of plastic. Typically disliked in the community due to its lack of breathability compared to cotton, but still currently the most used fabric by brands. Raschel lace: type of netted lace used most often in older pieces or by the brand Marble. Sometimes frowned upon due to the association it has with replicas in the early 2000s that used cheaply produced Raschel lace to cut corners.
Risa: one of Angelic Pretty's current models. Here she is modeling Happy Treat Party OP.
Punkuma: bear mascot previously used by Metamorphose Temps de Fille
Rabbitch: rabbit mascot previously used by Metamorphose Temps De Fille.
Rocking Horse Shoes: ballerina shoe inspired footwear that uses a wooden or rubber sole and often includes ties that wrap around the leg. They also typically feature a cut out on the back of the sole. Designs are based on Vivienne Westwood's Rocking Horse Ballerina Shoes. Lolitas most often wear Bodyline's Rocking horse Shoes, or Vivienne Westwood's. Commonly abbreviated as RHS.
Sax: light blue
seagull: Someone who hangs out on CGL.
shipping service: A service that will receive packages on your behalf and then ship them to you. Typically used to consolidate multiple packages to save on shipping or to buy from brands that do not ship outside of their country.
shopping service: service that will buy, consolidate, and ship orders on your behalf from stores that do not accept orders from customers outside of their country.
tea parties: type of lolita shoe.
Tenso: most often used Japanese shipping service.
trim: lower layer of a dress that contains ruffled fabric or lace.
tulle lace: most commonly used lace in lolita nowadays.
UTK: Under the Knee socks
usakumya: a mascot of Baby the Stars Shine Bright, typically paired with Kumya. visualkei: J-fashion style that was worn by specific bands, including Malice Mizer and Dir en Grey.
wishlist: a list of items someone wishes to own and is searching for in the secondhand market.
WTB: Want To Buy, the listing is intended to beckon sellers who are selling that item.
